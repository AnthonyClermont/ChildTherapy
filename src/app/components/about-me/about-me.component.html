Who am I?

Hi I’m Samantha, usually known as Sam.

I’m a qualified teacher and therapist. I have an in depth knowledge of child development and work closely with parents, teachers and outside agencies to ensure the best outcome for children. I apply a cross theoretical, integrative and holistic therapeutic play approach and work non-directively, alongside the introduction of more directed interventions when appropriate. I have a deep respect for children, I’m warm and accepting.

I have experience of working therapeutically with children experiencing a diverse range of emotional/ behavioural/ socialand mental health difficulties including domestic violence, neglect, bereavement, separation, attachment, autism, selective mutism, anxiety, and more. I offer a safe, confidential space for children to explore their experiences, allowing for self-expression and gentle exploration of difficulties whilst promoting independence, self-esteem and self-regulation.

I’m qualified with a Post Graduate Certificate in Therapeutic Play Skills, with distinction and also hold a Post Graduate Certificate in Education (PGCE). I have completed an Advanced Professional Trauma course with PESI. I’m a registered member of Play Therapy UK (PTUK) and adhere to their ethical framework. PTUK is accredited by the Professional Standards Authority (PSA). I attend regular supervision, have an advanced DBS, professional indemnity and public liability insurance and registered with ICO for data protection purposes.

I’m married with two children and love spending time with my family. I enjoy practising yoga, paddle boarding, ballroom dancing and hiking.
